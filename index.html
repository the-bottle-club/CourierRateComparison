Extended Services Summary

Evri (MHI) is the clear winner for all extended regions. DHL Zone C (£10.00) is slightly cheaper than DPD (£10.98) for Highlands & Islands and Northern Ireland on the first parcel, but DPD wins on Channel Islands (£14.03 vs £15.00 DHL Zone D). DHL Zone D is the most expensive for Isle of Man and Channel Islands. InPost and Royal Mail do not cover any extended regions at the quoted rates.

Key Surcharge Risks to Note

  • DPD fuel & energy is currently 18.7% (eff. 16 March 2026) — applied as a percentage of the base rate per shipment. This changes regularly; check dpd.co.uk/fuel before finalising any cost models.
  • DHL fuel & road is currently 24.5% (April 2026, up to 31.5kg) — indexed monthly to Polish diesel prices and applied on top of base rates. This is the highest fuel surcharge of all carriers reviewed at this time.
  • Royal Mail applies both an 11% fuel surcharge (eff. 5 Jan 2026) and a 5p green surcharge per item (eff. 31 Mar 2025) to Tracked 24 & 48 contract customers. Neither has been confirmed as part of the current deal negotiation, but both are standard for contract accounts.
  • Evri's £50 collection surcharge fires automatically if volume drops below 140 parcels per collection. In periods of lower volume this could be a significant unexpected cost.
  • InPost currently has the cleanest surcharge profile — just 5% fuel on top of the agreed rates. No congestion or green levy.
  • DPD congestion charge of £0.95 per shipment is higher than DHL's £0.75 — relevant at scale — but DHL's fuel percentage (24.5%) is currently significantly higher than DPD's (18.7%).
